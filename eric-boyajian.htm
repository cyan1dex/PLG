Eric Boyajian

Mr. Boyajian’s practice focuses on labor and employment litigation and counseling, including areas such as:

  • Wage and Hour
  • Wrongful Termination
  • Trade Secrets and Unfair Competition
  • Harassment
  • Discrimination
  • Retaliation
  • Leaves of Absence
  • Privacy
  • Contracts

Previously, from 2007 through February 2012, Mr. Boyajian served as the General Counsel for an industrial bakery, Global Bakeries, Inc. There, Mr. Boyajian counseled on all issues related to the bakery, beyond labor and employment/human resources issues. Mr. Boyajian negotiated and drafted numerous contracts; he advised on how to comply with federal food safety laws; and he litigated against customers, vendors and distributors. From 2005 to 2007, Mr. Boyajian was an Associate Attorney in the labor and employment department of Lewis Brisbois Bisgaard & Smith LLP. There, Mr. Boyajian handled all issues, as mentioned above, except his focus was on defense litigation. During this time, Mr. Boyajian had two articles published: Policy on Refusing to Hire Smokers: Why a California Employer Will Likely Be Able to Enforce Such a Policy, The Job Description, The Newsletter of the DRI Employment Law Committee, Winter 2007, at 37; and Federal Rules of Civil Procedure Adapt to Modern E-Business, Lewis Brisbois Bisgaard & Smith LLP’s Employment Newsletter, Spring 2007, at 10.

Prior to his time as a labor and employment litigator, Mr. Boyajian held clerkships with the following institutions: Albert Abkarian & Associates; L.A. District Attorney’s Office (Preliminary Hearings Unit and Hardcore Gang Unit); National Association of Securities Dealers; California Superior Court, Malibu Courthouse – Judge Lawrence Mira; and United States District Court, Central District of California – Judge Dikran Tevrizian. Mr. Boyajian received his Juris Doctor from Pepperdine School of Law. He also received the honor of being on the Dean’s List; the Witkin Award for Academic Excellence; and the CALI Excellence for the Future Award. Mr. Boyajian received his Bachelor of Arts from the University of Southern California in International Relations, with a Minor in Business Administration.

Mr. Boyajian also regularly volunteers in a charitable community organization called the Armenian Youth Association of the Armenian General Benevolent Union. He was on the committee for the AGBU-AYA 2012 World Games; from 2006 to 2007 he was the chairman of the AGBU-AYA San Fernando Valley Chapter; and from 1994 to 2000 he fulfilled various roles related to the AGBU-AYA San Fernando Valley Boy Scouts Troop 440, such as Committee Chairman, Local Commissioner, Scoutmaster, Asst. Scoutmaster and Cubmaster.

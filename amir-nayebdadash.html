Amir Nayebdadash

Mr. Nayebdadash is one of the founding partners of PLG. His practice focuses on prosecuting businesses who violate employment laws in single plaintiff and wage and hour class actions. Prior to establishing PLG, Mr. Nayebdadash worked in the Los Angeles office of Littler Mendelson, P.C., the largest labor and employment law firm in the country. While at Littler, Mr. Nayebdadash gained valuable experience defending some of the most recognized companies in California and nationally. Although Mr. Nayebdadash still advises smaller and mid-size businesses in their employment practices, he now uses his knowledge and experience of the inner workings of those companies to successfully litigate claims on behalf of aggrieved and mistreated employees. Mr. Nayebdadash also has extensive jury and bench trial experience and has won each matter he has tried.

Mr. Nayebdadash obtained his undergraduate degree from UCLA where he graduated cum laude with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Philosophy. He continued his studies at King’s College School of Law in London, England where he obtained his Master of Arts degree with merit honors in International Relations. Mr. Nayebdadash continued his studies at Pepperdine University School of Law where he obtained his Jurist Doctor degree in only two and a half years with a certification in Alternate Dispute Resolution from the acclaimed Straus Institute for Dispute Resolution. During law school, Mr. Nayebdadash returned to London where he worked as a legal intern for McDermott, Will and Emery’s London office, and Eversheds, LLP, a prominent multi-national law firm.

After law school, Mr. Nayebdadash joined one of the preeminent defense trial lawyers in Southern California, the Law Offices of David J. Weiss, where he helped Mr. Weiss expand his labor and employment practice whose receipts thereafter exceeded all other practice areas. Recognizing his skill and leadership, Littler recruited Mr. Nayebdadash to join its Los Angeles office.

Mr. Nayebdadash is active the Iranian-American community through his commitment to the Iranian-American Bar Association (IABA) and membership in several other Iranian-American organizations. He recently served as the Representative on the National Board of Directors for the IABA and remains an active member in the organization and the community.
